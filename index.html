refrag.js
breathing new life into URL hashes

Do you remember why the hash sign (#) was being put into URLs?...

No, it wasn't "because of AJAX".
Not for Google Analytics either.

Hash was followed by a fragment identifier. It referred to portion of document.
If you opened the URL, you were taken directly to this portion.

No scrolling, no searching. Right to the point.

So, what happened?

On the target side, supporting fragment identifiers was cumbersome. It required placement of anchors - such as <a name="foo"/> - for the fragments we wanted to have links to. That was an additional effort which was often non-trivial.

But even if author undertook this effort, results were hardly impressive. You could still only link to fragments that had anchors placed - usually headers or sections. What if you wanted more granularity, like paragraphs or sentences?... If the anchor wasn't there - well, tough luck.
